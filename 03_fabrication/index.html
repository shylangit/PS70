<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 3: Hand Tools and Fabrication</h3> <h4>Kinetic Sculpture</h4> <p> Drafting </p> <p> For this week's assignment, I had several ideas: </p> <ul> <li> Boat/ocean </li> <li> Flying Pokemon </li> <li> Ferris wheel </li> </ul> <p> I decided to make the ferris wheel! </p> <p> Step 1: Sketching </p> <p> To start my design process, I began by hand-sketching some ideas and design for the ferris wheel. After some rough idea drawing, I translated my sketch to Fusion and made some adjustments. </p> <p> Step 2: Prototyping </p> <p> Before I cut out my design, I first made a smaller prototype to play around with the spinning mechanism. I sketched and cut out a smaller 6-inch circle, an attachment for the motor, and some temporary legs for the wheel. I decided to use a gear motor to spin my ferris wheel, since the spinning part isn't a circle, so it would be easier to get it to spin my structure. </p> <p> After cutting out my pieces, I got the protoype spinning with the motor. I made some slight adjustments along the way to the dimensions of the motor attachment so it would better fit the shape of the motor. </p> <p> Step 3: Wheel Construction </p> <p> After making my prototype spin, I made some minor adjustments to the dimensions of my full sketch on Fusion accordingly. Once ready, I cut the parts for my ferris wheel in the laser cutter. </p> <p> Afterwards, I started assembling the pieces of my ferris wheel together. This is where I ran into most of my design problems. I had some issues with aligning the spinning rod properly after assembling the legs. There were also some slight issues with the motor slipping out of the motor attachment. </p> <p> Final Product: </p> <img src="ferris-wheel.gif" width="350"> <p> Improvements: </p> <p> For the next iteration of this project, these are the changes I would like to make: </p> <ul> <li> Adjust dimensions of cardboard legs to better account for the thickness of the cardboard. </li> <li> Figure out a way to make the motor stay in the motor mount better. </li> <li> Be more precise while assembling the structure to ensure proper alignment of pieces. </li> </ul> </div>
