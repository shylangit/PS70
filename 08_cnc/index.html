<div class="textcontainer"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 8: CNC Milling</h3> <h4>Assignment: Make Something With CNC</h4> <p> For this week's assignment, I wanted to make a functional design. I landed on making a jewelry tray. My inspiration for this week was an organic design. I went with a koi pond design. </p> <p> To create this project, I first modeled my design on Fusion.</p> <iframe src="https://a360.co/3WvybT2" width="800" height="600" frameborder="0" allowfullscreen> </iframe> <p> To do this, I drew the the shape of the pond, and then imported vector images of the koi fish onto my sketch. After, I drew the other elements of the pond using Fusion's sketch tools. </p> <p> Next, I loaded my design into the CNC software and prepared it to be cut. After doing so, I noticed some issues with extra lines on vector image fish, so, I went back and cleaned up my Fusion sketch. </p> <img src="koi_pond_wooden.jpg" alt="koi pond unsanded" style="width:600px" /> <p> After sanding: </p> <img src="pond-thumbnail.jpg" alt="koi pond" style="width:600px" /> <p> Reflections: </p> <p> Overall, I was happy with how the pond came out. I liked how the wood layering allowed for the water to be a different color then the other elements in the design. In future iterations, I would probably scale the internal elements in the pond up a little to allow them to cut a little cleaner (especially the water ripples, which I ended up sanding off) and smooth some of the edges on the fin/tail design. I would also like to add more elements into the pond. </p> <h4>Molding and Casting</h4> <p> For the molding and casting portion of this week, I decided to 3D print my cat scan from week 5 and make a mold of it. </p> <img src="3D_print_cat_front.jpg" alt="3D print cat front" style="width:250px" /> <img src="3D_print_cat_side.jpg" alt="3D print cat side" style="width:250px" /> <p> I decided to use vacuum forming to create a mold of the cat, and then I used plaster to cast it. </p> <img src="plaster_mold.jpg" alt="plaster mold" style="width:550px" /> <p> (Shared vacuum sheet with Kieran) </p> <p> Here is the finished plaster cat! </p> <img src="plaster_cat.jpg" alt="plaster cat" style="width:550px" /> </div>
